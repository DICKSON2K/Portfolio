Japanese Spitz Website

My Role: Software Developer

As a software developer, I was responsible for the complete lifecycle of the web application. This included planning, designing, coding, testing, and maintaining the website. Below are some screenshots that highlight different aspects of the website.

This is the homepage of the website, primarily designed for large screens. The site aims to give Japanese Spitz dogs a new home by providing detailed information and appealing visuals.

This screenshot showcases the mobile layout of the homepage. The responsive design ensures that users on smaller screens, such as smartphones and tablets, have an equally satisfying experience. The layout adapts to different screen sizes, maintaining functionality and aesthetics.

The adoption page is a critical part of the website, providing users with detailed profiles of available dogs. It includes photos, descriptions, and adoption procedures. This page is designed to be user-friendly and informative, making it easy for potential adopters to find their perfect companion.

The contact page is designed to facilitate communication between the users and the organization. It includes a contact form, location information, and social media links. This ensures that users can easily reach out for inquiries, support, or to start the adoption process.
